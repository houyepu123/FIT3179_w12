The change in total annual CO2 emission from 1750 to 2021

The graph traces annual CO₂ emissions from 1770 to 2010, highlighting humanity's escalating carbon footprint. Emissions were relatively low during the early Industrial Revolution, around 1770, but saw a dramatic surge in the 20th century due to rapid industrialization and reliance on fossil fuels. This uptick, especially pronounced post-1960s, correlates with rising global temperatures, emphasizing the human role in climate change. The data underscores the pressing need for sustainable energy solutions, balancing economic growth with environmental responsibility.
