Bubble plot of GDP vs CO2 Emissions of 2018

The bubble plot illustrating GDP versus CO₂ emissions provides a compelling visual insight into the economic and environmental dynamics of nations. Each bubble's size is indicative of the population, offering a third dimension to the data. While numerous countries display varying degrees of economic growth and carbon outputs, one country stands prominently: China. With its meteoric economic rise, China not only showcases one of the highest GDPs but also tops the chart in CO₂ emissions, underscoring the challenges and responsibilities it faces in the global effort toward sustainable development.

Scatter plot of GDP per capita vs Annual CO2 emissions per capita

The scatter plot provides an insightful visualization of GDP per capita against Annual CO₂ emission for various nations. Each data point represents a country, with its position determined by its economic standing and environmental impact. Notably, Qatar emerges as a distinct outlier. It boasts the highest GDP per capita, demonstrating its significant economic stature. However, this economic prosperity comes at a price, as Qatar also leads in Annual CO₂ emission, highlighting the environmental challenges accompanying its wealth.
